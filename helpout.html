Volunteering

Books Through Bars is an all-volunteer organization. Our volunteers range from students to retirees, hail from all parts of Philadelphia, its suburbs and even other states, and come from every walk of life. We’re a lively and eclectic group united for a single worthy cause.

Book Picking and Packing

The easiest way to get involved with Books Through Bars is to participate in one of our regularly scheduled book packing activities. You will read letters from prisoners requesting books, search through our library of donated books for a good match, and package the books for mailing.

Letter processing: anytime, from home

We receive hundreds of book request letters each week. Each one needs to be read, sorted and logged into our database. With some training required, letter loggers can easily do this task from home—all you need is a computer and internet connection. Trips to our office is needed to pick up and drop off letters.

Driving

We always need help picking up and/or delivering book donations. You will need to supply the car and gas. You can make this a regular part of your week or respond to individual calls for drivers.

Library, administrative, outreach

Book organizing: help us sort and shelve the loads of donated books we get each week

Office: Respond to non-book requests, contact prisons, update databases, send thank-you letters, work on “special fills,” help us keep our space clean and organized, and other tasks.

Miscellaneous: join the fundraising committee, host/oversee book packing sessions, staff fundraising events. Have other special skills and talents you want to share? Let us know.

We welcome all people and any skills you may have to offer, whether it’s for an evening or a longer commitment. Join us today!

Weekly Volunteer Hours

Join us on Tuesday nights 7:30–9:30 PM or the 1st and 3rd Saturday of every month, 11:00 AM–2:00 PM.

Our walk-in sessions are 18 and over only. Contact us to arrange service learning events for kids and young adults. We welcome groups, but ask that you contact us ahead of time so we can accommodate you.

Volunteering for groups

BTB hosts a variety of groups, from schools and universities to interfaith groups, community service organizations and even birthday parties. We welcome groups of up to 15 or so people. For groups with youth, we require a chaperone for every five kids.

You will read letters from prisoners requesting books, search through our library of donated books for a good match, and package the books for mailing. For special service groups, a BTB volunteer will also give a short presentation and lead a discussion about incarceration and educational systems, and how BTB works to intervene in those cycles.

Donating

books, time, knowledge? whatever you can offer!

Your donation makes a direct difference. As an all-volunteer nonprofit, we rely entirely on our dedicated supporters to provide money to pay for expenses, volunteer to get things done, and of course, donate books! All donations go directly towards supporting our programs, and are also tax-deductible.

We are grateful to the individuals, groups, organizations and foundations that have given all the above to make our work possible. We are especially thankful to our incarcerated donors, whose hard-won cash and stamp donations make what we do worth it.

Other ways to contribute

There are many ways you can help out

The Jailhouse Lawyer’s Handbook

Published by the Center for Constitutional Rights and the National Lawyer’s Guild, the Jailhouse Lawyer’s Manual is a 158-page handbook that provides guidelines for incarcerated people who wish to file a federal lawsuit addressing poor conditions or abuse and general information about the American legal system.

We frequently receive requests for this manual. If you or your organization are interested in either printing it yourself or sponsoring a print run, please contact us so we can discuss how many to make and how they should be printed and bound.

Special projects & supplies

Hold a book drive or fundraiser at your school, business, house of worship, etc. Print out our most needed books list (PDF) and hang it next to the box. If you can’t bring the books in, contact us; we may be able to arrange a pick up.

Post our volunteer flyer on your local community board, school, workplace, religious center, community hall, or local cafe. Download it here. (PDF)

Host an event to educate your community about the prison system: you supply the space and we’ll supply the speaker! Email us today to set this up.

Donate office supplies. We always need copy paper, packing tape, paper clips, post-its, and business and manila envelopes.

Donate a laptop! We could really use one, preferably not more than two or three years old. Don't worry. In the PC vs Mac war, we're neutral.

Spread the word! Post our most needed books flyer at your school, workplace, religious center, community board, or local store. Consider doing a book drive; we may be able to pick up the books.
